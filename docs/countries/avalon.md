# Avalon

!!! example "Constitutional Monarchy"
**Population:** 65 million
**Legislature:** National Assembly (650 seats)
**System:** Westminster-style constitutional monarchy
**Elections:** Every 4 game years

**About Avalon:**

Avalon is the flagship country in Lawmaker, featuring:

- Large legislature (650 seats) - Room for many parties
- Hereditary Crown - The Crown of Avalon can be claimed through the monarchy nomination system
- Royal constitutional power - The Crown initially holds the power to grant pardons
- Active player community
- Established political traditions
- Complex coalition dynamics

**Ideal for:**

- Players who want lots of competition
- Those who enjoy coalition building
- Players interested in Westminster-style systems
- Players who want to pursue a royal dynasty as an alternative to normal party politics

## The Crown of Avalon

Avalon has a hereditary monarchy: **The Crown of Avalon**.

When the throne is vacant, each active party may nominate one activist for the Crown. Avalon uses the **oldest party** selection method, so the nomination from the oldest eligible nominating party wins when the nomination window closes. If no one nominates a candidate, the vacancy is extended.

The winning party becomes dormant while it reigns. It loses its seats, cabinet offices, international bloc memberships, and Political Power, then plays as the Crown until abdication or inactivity removal.

See [Hereditary Monarchy](../gameplay/monarchy.md) for the full rules.
